Marine

Marines are disciplined warriors trained for combat in any environment. They execute military doctrine with precision, combining versatility with overwhelming firepower.

Level 1 Combat Doctrine

You adopt a combat doctrine that shapes how you fight. You can change your active doctrine as a bonus action on your turn. Choose one doctrine at a time:

  • Assault Doctrine: +2 bonus to damage rolls on your turn
  • Defense Doctrine: +1 AC and advantage on opportunity attack saving throws
  • Mobility Doctrine: Your speed increases by 10 feet and standing up costs only 5 feet of movement

At level 11, you may maintain two doctrines simultaneously.

Level 1 Suppressive Fire

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you may force it to make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Strength or Dexterity modifier). On a failure, the target has disadvantage on its next attack roll before the end of its next turn.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

Level 2 Military Grit

You can push beyond normal limits. On your turn, you can take one additional action. Once you use this feature, you must finish a short or long rest before using it again.

Level 3 Marine Corps

Choose a Marine corps that reflects your specialized training:

  • Shock Trooper - Brutal melee warrior
  • Infantry Gunner - strategic ranged combat
  • Siege Marine - one man tank

Your archetype grants features at levels 3, 7, 10, 14, and 18.

Level 5 Extra Attack

Make two attacks from one attack action.

Level 6 Field Conditioning

You gain proficiency in Constitution saving throws if you don't already have it. If you do, you instead gain advantage on Constitution saving throws against exhaustion, poison, and environmental hazards.

Level 9 Indomitable Advance

Opportunity attacks against you are made with disadvantage, and you can move through hostile creature spaces as if they were difficult terrain.

Level 11 Improved Suppression

When a creature fails its save against your Suppressive Fire, its speed is reduced to 0 until the start of its next turn.

Level 13 Veteran Reflexes

You gain advantage on initiative rolls and cannot be surprised while conscious.

Level 15 Combat Endurance

When you drop to 0 hit points but aren't killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. Once used, you must finish a long rest before using this feature again.

Level 17 Relentless Assault

If you reduce a hostile creature to 0 hit points on your turn, you can make one additional weapon attack as part of the same action.

Level 20 War Machine

At the start of combat, you gain temporary hit points equal to your Marine level + Constitution modifier, and your first attack each turn automatically benefits from Suppressive Fire without expending a use.
